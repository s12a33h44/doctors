PRECAUTIONS

Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds.

If soap and water are not available use hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol.

Our touching your face nose eyes.

If someone inside your home becomes sick, they should be tested for COVID-19.

If they test positive, they should isolate from other household members.

Cover your nose and mouth when around others.

Everyone should wear a mask or other face coverings in public settings.

SYMPTOMS

Symptoms begin 2 to 14 days after you come into contact with the virus many people who have mild infections recover within two weeks more severe cases tend to last three to six weeks.

Fever or chills, Cough, Fatigue.

Fatigue, Muscle or body aches, Headache.

Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing.

New loss of taste or smell, Sore throat.

Congestion or runny nose, Nausea or vomiting, Diarrhea.

TREATMENT

Rest. It can make you feel better and may speed your recovery.

Stay home. Don’t go to work, school, or public places.

Drink fluid. You lose more water when you’re sick. Dehydration can make symptoms worse and cause other health problems.

Monitor. If your symptoms get worse, call your doctor Right away don’t go to the office without calling.

Ask your doctor about over the counter medicines that may help to lower your fever.

Try to stay in one place in your home.

Don’t share dishes or cups or eating Utensils.
